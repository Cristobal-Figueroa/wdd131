Frequently Asked Questions

Many Chilean ingredients can be found in specialized Latin American stores or online retailers. We recommend checking:

  • Latin American or South American specialty grocery stores
  • Online marketplaces like Amazon, Mercado Latino, or Tienda.com
  • International sections of larger supermarkets
  • Local Chilean or South American restaurants may also sell some ingredients

On our Ingredients page, we offer detailed information about where to find specific items and possible substitutes that work well in Chilean recipes.

Absolutely! We understand that some Chilean ingredients can be difficult to find outside the country. Here are some general substitution guidelines:

  • Merkén: Mix smoked paprika with cayenne pepper and ground coriander
  • Ají chileno: Substitute with red jalapeños or fresno peppers
  • Cochayuyo: Use kombu or other dried seaweed
  • Mote: Pearl barley or wheat berries work well
  • Chilote potatoes: Small waxy potatoes like fingerlings or new potatoes

Each recipe on our site includes specific substitution suggestions that maintain the authentic flavor profile while using more accessible ingredients.

We welcome contributions from our community! To share your Chilean recipes:

  1. Fill out our contact form above
  2. Select "Share Recipe" from the subject dropdown
  3. Complete the recipe details including title, region of origin, ingredients, and preparation instructions
  4. If possible, include information about the history or cultural significance of the dish

Our culinary team will review your submission and may contact you for additional details or clarification. If selected, your recipe will be featured on our site with full credit to you (unless you prefer to remain anonymous). We particularly value family recipes and regional variations that showcase Chile's diverse culinary heritage.
